Research different types of internet connections (e.g., broadband, fiber, satellite)
and list their pros and cons.

1. Broadband (DSL & Cable)

(a) DSL (Digital Subscriber Line)
Uses: Telephone lines

Pros:
Widely available (even in smaller towns)
Affordable
You can use phone and internet at the same time

Cons:
Slower than cable and fiber
Speed drops the farther you are from the exchange
Not ideal for heavy streaming or gaming

(b) Cable Internet

Uses: Coaxial cable (same as cable TV)

Pros:
Faster than DSL
Good for streaming, gaming, and video calls
Widely available in cities

Cons:
Speed may slow down during peak hours (shared bandwidth)
Slightly more expensive than DSL

2. Fiber Optic Internet

Uses: Fiber-optic cables (light signals)

Pros:
Extremely fast speeds (up to Gbps)
Very reliable and stable
Best for online gaming, 4K streaming, cloud work

Cons:
Limited availability (mostly urban areas)
Expensive installation in some regions

3. Satellite Internet

Uses: Satellites in space

Pros:
Available in remote and rural areas
No need for cable or phone lines

Cons:

4. Mobile / Cellular Internet (4G / 5G)

Uses: Mobile network towers

Pros:
Portable and convenient
Easy setup (hotspot or dongle)
5G offers very high speeds

Cons:
Data limits can be costly
Speed depends on signal strength
Not as stable as fiber or cable

5. Dial-Up (Older Technology)

Uses: Telephone line

Pros:
Very low cost
Works where no other internet is available

Cons:
Extremely slow
Cannot use phone and internet simultaneously
Mostly outdated
